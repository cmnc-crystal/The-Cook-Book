Gyoza

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • ready-made gyoza skins, defrosted if frozen
  • 2-3 tbsp vegetable oil

For the Filling

  • 4 spring onions, ends trimmed, roughly chopped
  • 2 large leaves of Savoy or pointed cabbages, hard stem removed, roughly chopped
  • a 1½ cm piece of ginger, peeled and chopped
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 50g water chestnuts, about 5 (drained weight)
  • 2 tsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp cooking saké
  • ½ tsp sesame oil
  • 140g minced pork or chicken

For Soy Dipping Sauce

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 tsp sesame oil

How to Make this Gyoza

  1. Put the spring onions, cabbage, ginger and garlic in a food processor, and whizz to a fine mix.
  2. Add the water chestnuts and pulse to chop, but not too finely – these will add a nice crunchy texture.
  3. Add the soy sauce, oyster sauce, sake, sesame oil and a pinch of salt, and whizz again.
  4. Tip the ingredients into a bowl and add the minced pork or chicken. Mix by hand until well combined.
    Chill until ready to use.
  5. Have a pot of water to hand. Sprinkle the cornflour onto a plate.
  6. To assemble the gyoza, hold the dumpling skin in the palm of one hand and
    put a heaped teaspoon of the filling onto the centre of the skin.
  7. Dip your finger in the water and wipe around the edge of the skin – this will moisten it and
    help the edges stick together.
  8. Bring the edges of the skin together. Pinch pleats along one side, then press each pleat against
    the opposite flat side of the skin. With each pinch make sure that you are sealing the parcel and
    keeping the filling in the centre.
  9. Put each gyoza onto the plate dusted with cornflour. Can be covered with cling film and chilled for up to 8 hrs.
  10. Cook the gyoza in batches. Heat a non-stick frying pan with 1 tbsp vegetable oil.
    Brush off any excess cornflour from the bases of the dumplings.
  11. Fry the gyoza on one side only – don’t turn them over, you just want one crispy side.
    They should be golden brown after about 2 mins.
  12. Add a good splash of water to the pan and cover with a steaming lid or a large sheet of foil with
    a few holes poked in the top.
  13. Cook over a medium heat for 3-5 mins until the water has evaporated and the gyoza filling is cooked through.
    Set aside while you cook the rest.
  14. Mix all the dipping sauce ingredients and serve alongside the gyoza in dipping bowls.
